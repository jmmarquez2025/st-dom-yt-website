# PRODUCT

## What this is
The public website for **St. Dominic Catholic Church**, a parish in Youngstown, Ohio,
served by the **Dominican Friars** (Order of Preachers) since 1923. It is a real, rooted
parish — not a product, startup, or SaaS app. The building is a historic Romanesque church.

## Users
- **Parishioners** — all ages, skewing older; many visit on phones to check Mass/Confession times.
- **Visitors & seekers** — people new to the area, returning to the faith, or exploring Catholicism.
- **Spanish-speaking community** — served by the Hispanic Ministry; the site is fully bilingual (EN/ES) and must stay at parity.
- Primary jobs-to-be-done: find Mass & Confession times, plan a first visit, learn about sacraments, give, contact the office.

## Brand & tone
- **Reverent, warm, plain, pastoral, human.** Catholic and Dominican in character.
- Sounds like a parish bulletin written by a thoughtful pastor — not a marketing team.
- **Less text is better.** The pastor's explicit feedback: much of the prior copy "screamed AI." Cut filler, hedging, and hospitality-brochure boilerplate.
- Bilingual, accessible (older / low-vision readers), mobile-first.

## Anti-references (what it must NOT look or sound like)
- Generic AI-generated landing pages and SaaS marketing sites.
- Glassmorphism-heavy startup aesthetics; Linear/Vercel/Stripe clones.
- "Hero metrics" stat banners (e.g. "4 Masses · 2 Languages · 5 Friars").
- Gradient text, rainbow gradients, neon-on-dark "tech" palettes.
- Boilerplate copy: "vibrant community," "we'd love to welcome you," "dedicated team… spiritual guidance, support, and resources," "come be a part of our family."
- Over-animated scroll gimmicks that call attention to themselves.

## Strategic principles
1. Clarity and reverence over cleverness.
2. Accuracy of practical info (times, addresses) is sacred — never let decoration obscure it.
3. Accessibility (WCAG AA) is a requirement, not a nice-to-have — the congregation skews older.
4. EN and ES must say the same thing, equally well.
5. The design should feel hand-made for this specific parish, not assembled from a template.
